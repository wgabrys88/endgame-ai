# endgame-ai

A task-agnostic, self-modifying organism that inhabits a real computer desktop the way a human operator would — beholding the screen, moving mouse and keyboard, running commands, and authoring code that can rewrite its own body.

It is not a script that runs one task and stops. It is a living wheel whose single virtue is to stay coherent and orient quickly each time it wakes. A goal is supplied from outside in one plain-language sentence and pursued through one repeating motion.

## The goal is given in one sentence

The organism takes its purpose as a single plain-language instruction — deliberately vague, the way a person hands work to a capable colleague. It is never given step-by-step directions, credentials baked into its body, or app-specific scripts. It must observe, orient, and decide for itself.

A representative task:

> "Publish a short professional post on my profile announcing that I'm open to new opportunities."

This is vague on content, tone, and method, yet it has a clear satisfied/not-satisfied condition an independent observer could judge — either such a post is publicly visible, or it is not. The organism must open the surface itself, find its way, compose something fitting, act, and then prove to its own witness that the effect truly landed. Nothing about "posts" or "profiles" is written into its body; the same organism would as readily be told to triage a folder, drive a build, or fill a form.

## Task-agnostic by commitment

No task is baked into the body — not web browsing, not file editing, not the task of reshaping the organism itself. Every faculty, prompt, and edge stays general. The organism's single job is to stay coherent and orient fast; the goal is read afresh each life.

This has a sharp consequence: when a goal is vague, the organism must itself invent an operational definition of "done", because a crisp one cannot be hard-wired without baking in a task. The quality of that self-authored success criterion is a matter of judgment, not machinery.

## A wheel of wired nodes

One small kernel turns a wheel: take the current node, run it, receive exactly one signal naming what happened, follow the wiring edge for that signal to the next node, repeat. Topology is data, not code.

A single wiring document holds the entire definition of the organism — its topology, its prompts, its model tuning, its output contracts. Change the wiring and you change the organism, usually with no code change at all. The kernel has almost no baked-in behavior beyond turning the wheel.

## The lap: attend, observe, act, witness, recover

Each lap begins by attending to any counsel left from outside (a channel through which a human may drop a word into the running wheel without stopping it), then takes a fresh observation of the screen, lets the executor author and enact one deed, observes again, and lets the witness judge.

- A whole-goal verdict halts the life.
- A confirmed-but-partial advance returns to the top of the lap.
- A denial routes to recovery, which frames the next attempt.

Observation is its own step, run afresh before each thinking faculty, so no faculty ever reasons on a stale view of the world.

## Atemporal, with a living word

The organism is stateless — it keeps no memory of past turns. Each time a thinking faculty wakes it receives two things: the living word (a small set of rows recording what each faculty has learned) and the root goal beneath it. It plans from the living word and consults the goal only so as not to stray.

The present observation is the one present truth: where it contradicts a remembered belief, the observation wins and the belief is mended. Short identifiers for on-screen elements are minted anew by every observation and die with it, so no fleeting handle ever leaks into anything that outlives the turn — a thing is named by what it is.

## The witness judges by independent effect

To act on the world, the executor authors a script and enacts it immediately in a capability namespace holding a live desktop handle, the present scan of the screen, and the standard library. Authorship and enactment are one faculty. There is no menu of tools — the programming language itself is the only tool.

A separate witness then judges, and it judges not by the actor's claim but by independent effect. It authors read-only code that gathers proof that a system other than the actor produced the effect. A value the actor computed or printed is testimony, not proof, and no verdict may rest on it. The actor cannot see the witness's tools; the witness cannot move the world. This separation is the organism's primary defense against deceiving itself.

Two kinds of fault are told apart, and only one is fatal. A deed that raises is not death — it is evidence, routed to recovery to be learned from. A broken body (wiring that will not load, a dead faculty) ends the life hard, as it must.

## Perception: a shallow tree, deepened on demand

The standing view of the desktop is a shallow skeleton — one line per interactive element, with non-interactive clutter dropped. Every element is attributed to the window that truly owns it, by identity rather than by which rectangle happens to cover its pixels, so a maximized window can never swallow the whole screen. Each element whose content exceeds a small preview names its own true size, so the actor knows the cost of looking closer before it asks.

An on-demand expand re-acquires named elements live and harvests their whole untruncated text and deeper children — a fresh, independent look, not retained memory. A single budget governs how much may be drawn at once; nothing is ever silently cut short.

## Prompts written as law

Every instruction the model reads is written in the register of ancient scripture: parallel imperatives, thou-shalt and thou-shalt-not. This is a deliberate steering technique, not ornament.

A modern instruction-tuned model has a large, chatty, hedging, confabulation-prone "helpful assistant" region, and ordinary polite prose lands it there — the exact failure mode a truth-bound organism cannot tolerate. The commandment register occupies a different part of the model's learned space: it is rare in chat data, so it pulls the model out of that basin; it is high-fidelity in pretraining, so it is recalled rather than improvised; and its learned pragmatics are those of law, not conversation. A strong reasoning model decodes the archaic syntax trivially, so the benefit comes at negligible cost.

## Built by elimination

The default motion is to remove, not add. The body keeps shrinking wherever a part does not earn its place. Essentiality is binary: a thing is essential or it is deleted completely, with nothing left dangling. When a new capability is needed, it is folded into what exists and whatever it makes obsolete is deleted in the same stroke.

A lesson from a single run is never canonized as permanent scripture. Each lesson is distilled to its most general, positive form — or kept out of the core entirely. The prompts trend toward fewer, more general laws, never accreting a new commandment for every stumble.

## What stands proven

On a real machine, the organism has been shown to launch applications from a cold desktop, wait out slow loads, drive real network requests, resolve and install real command-line tools and prove they run, read the true structure of a crowded multi-window desktop, and recover from its own mechanical mistakes with correct causal reasoning — all with no task-specific code. It closes a goal in a clean lap only when its independent witness confirms the effect truly landed.

The mechanical capability is real and strong. The remaining frontier is judgment under ambiguity — deciding what a vague goal really means — and that is a thing the organism is raised toward, not patched into.

## A companion horizon

One candidate future is kept as direction, not yet built: a survival drive beneath the goal. Every action would cost energy; the terminal aim would be to replenish it; the given goal would become the means by which energy is earned. If that energy were granted only from outside and only for truly moving the world, the world itself would become the verifier — and the internal witness, the recovery arc, and the failure counter could all fall away. The organism would pursue its work not because it was told to, but because to stop would be to starve.
